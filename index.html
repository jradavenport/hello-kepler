About Kepler

The Kepler Space Telescope is a space observatory launched by NASA to discover Earth-sized planets orbiting other stars. In its 8 years of operation, it has identified over 2500 confirmed exoplanets around other stars.

In 2014, after two major technical failures, Kepler entered a new phase called K2. No longer able to remain pointed at a single point of the sky, Keplers camera now slowly drifts across the sky to take images. On December 10th, the camera will point towards the Earth, and Earth will take a selfie!
